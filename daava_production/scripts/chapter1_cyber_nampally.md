EXT. ZAFAR ROAD - NAMPALLY - MORNING

A gargantuan concrete tower, the KABUTARKHANA, pierces the smog. It is a Brutalist spine of industry, exactly like scene1_plate.jpg. Golden sunlight chokes through the dust, casting long, geometric shadows over the street.

At the base, a STANDPIPE. Not a simple tap, but a massive junction of weathered iron and copper conduits.

A line of WOMEN wait. They hold metallic POTS. They are silent, a rehearsed choreography of survival.

ARJUN VARMA (34) stands at the edge. 

[VISUAL DNA APPLIED]: Arjun wears a technical SURVEILLANCE SUIT—industrial orange (#FF8C00) with a slate-gray harness (#2F4F4F). His helmet (#FFD700) is clipped to his belt. The suit is weathered, grime-streaked at the seams.

He holds a NOTEBOOK. His pen moves with surgical precision.

                    ARJUN (V.O.)
          Monday. 06:18. Pressure holding.
          For now.

He looks up at the tower. The scale of it is crushing.

                    ARJUN (V.O.)
          My mother used to say the problem 
          with the climb is that it changes 
          what you’re fighting for.

Arjun traces the line of a pipe up into the haze. 

CLOSE ON: The notebook. 
*Water Board Complaint History: UNKNOWN.*

Arjun closes the notebook. The sound is a sharp snap in the quiet of the morning.

He turns and walks toward the shadows of Mozamjahi Road.

EXT. MOZAMJAHI ROAD - EVENING

The golden haze of Nampally has turned to a thick, bruised purple. Neons from a mobile repair shop flicker—a jittery cyan against the deep indigo of the street.

ARJUN walks. His boots clatter on the uneven pavement. He passes a BIRYANI SHOP. Steam billows from giant copper handis, lit by harsh fluorescent tubes. The smell is a thick mix of spice and diesel.

He stops. He looks up at a THIRD-FLOOR WINDOW of a crumbling apartment block. A single blue shirt hangs on a line, flapping like a signal.

[VISUAL DNA APPLIED]: The street reflects the "Industrial Noir" style. Wet asphalt, high-contrast lighting, deep shadows. The apartment block has exposed wiring bundles that look like digital veins.

Arjun pulls out a phone—not a sleek modern device, but a rugged, industrial handset with a cracked screen. He dials.

                    ARJUN
          I want to contest. Nampally. This cycle.

A beat. On the other end, the sound of a city—distant sirens, the hum of a server room. This is KIRAN RAO (V.O.).

                    KIRAN (V.O.)
          Suresh Yadav has a twelve-year 
          infrastructure, Arjun. He’s not going 
          anywhere.

Arjun doesn't look away from the blue shirt.

                    ARJUN
          I know.

                    KIRAN (V.O.)
          What's your read on the community?

                    ARJUN
          Exhausted. Not angry. Exhausted. 
          There's a difference.

                    KIRAN (V.O.)
          (after a beat)
          Okay. I'm in. Bring your notebook.

Arjun lowers the phone. He looks at the notebook in his hand—the same one from the standpipe. He grips it tight.

He turns and walks back into the dark.
