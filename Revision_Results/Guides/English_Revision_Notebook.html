Lucy (Poem)
Q. Why does the poet compare Lucy to a Violet flower and a fair star?
The poet compares her to a Violet flower and a fair star. The comparison is appropriate because the violet flower beside a mossy stone is often hidden and remains unnoticed. Similarly, a fair star cannot be noticed when all other stars are shining, but is appreciated when it is the only one in the sky. Lucy was a woman whom few people knew and appreciated.
Q. What happened when Lucy died? How was the speaker affected?
When Lucy died, few people came to know of her death, but the poet was greatly affected. He felt a deep sense of loss as she was "the one" for him.
