Skeleton & Animal Movement
Q. Name the two girdles in the human skeleton?
Ans: The pelvic girdle and the pectoral girdle.
Q. Name the bones in the human arm?
Ans: The **humerus** is the bone found in the upper arm, and the **radius** and **ulna** are found in the lower arm.
Q. How do the following animals move?
1. Earthworm: It moves by using a set of muscles which contract and expand the body. It has soft hair-like projections called setae to grip the ground.

2. Snail: Snails move by creeping on a flat 'foot' underneath the body. The foot produces slimy mucus to make a slippery track.

3. Bird: Birds beat their wings against air to propel forward. Strong chest muscles move the wings up and down.

4. Snake: Snakes use their muscles and scales to move. They catch uneven surfaces and create a series of curves/loops.
